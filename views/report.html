Estimate the story points

 

With the exception of class assignments, no team member has significant programming experience and therefore it was difficult to estimate durations of the stories we planned to complete for Sprint-1.  Instead, we used a combination of a relative size vs. time estimate and planning poker (without the cards) to approximate story completion times.  

 

Each team member was asked to privately rank stories they felt the team would be able to complete in the coming week.  We then compared everyone’s list to see which stories were considered doable by all members and which were not.  Every story that was considered doable for the week was added to our sprint list and the rest were discussed to understand why they were not doable. For example, stories 4-8 (see above) were originally thought by some to be too difficult for the first sprint, but we then discussed the logistics of completing individual tasks. Through this conversation we realized that these tasks were achievable (we realized some team members had a better grasp of the story requirement) and so were added to Sprint-1 story log.  

 

For stories that were not included on anyone’s list, we decided we were going to check progress during our next sprint meeting.  We believed our first sprint would give everyone a better understanding of how long each story would take.  


 

Overall Review of Sprint 1

We used the first to sprint to get a lay of the land and set ourselves up for the journey ahead. Once we were familiar with the scope of the project and schedule of deliverables, we began to set up accounts in github and cloud9. In the beginning we had some teething troubles in setting up github and cloud9 accounts for a couple of our team members. However, with some masterful fine tweaking by Brian, we overcame the first of many to come hurdles. We then went through the user stories and distributed tasks based on current comfort level with coding of individual team members. While the work ahead in this week is ambitious, we will see how it goes and use the learnings in future sprints.

 

Future Plans

For next sprint, we will start adding actual functions to the project. Our plan is to complete the basic format of each page. Then, we will work on homepage and 'About Us' page including the logo. We are planning to hold another scrum meeting at the end of this week to analyze our progress and performance. During this meeting, we also plan to discuss the remaining backlog and will either use a relative sizing technique or planning poker.
